Radburn N.J.

The town of Radburn N.J. was designed in the late 1920's, in anticipation of the completion of the George Washington Bridge (e.g. G.W.B). The G.W.B. would link the suburbs of New Jersey, (one of them being Radburn N.J.) to the business centers in New York City (about 15-20 miles away). Being in Radburn is almost like stepping back in time. The forward thinking designs (circa late 1920's) remain a major part of Radburn today:

Radburn was designed with kids in mind. The purpose was to make pathways, parks, ball fields, swimming pools, and schools accessible without having to cross streets.
